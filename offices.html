STUDENT SUPPORT OFFICES

Resources for Student Support

Maize&Blue Cupboard

The mission at Maize and Blue Cupboard is to ensure all members of the University of Michigan community have access to healthy, nutritious, and nourishing food, including the skills and tools to prepare it. With just a swipe of your MCard, MBC provides free ingredients, kitchen and cookware, personal and household supplies, and quick connections to other campus resources

Student Life

Student Life facilitates transformational student experiences by advancing student learning and development, fostering well-being, and cultivating an inclusive and equitable campus community.

First Generation

The First-Generation Student Program at U-M supports and empowers first-generation college students by providing resources, community-building opportunities, and advocacy. Through initiatives and a dedicated office, the program helps increase student success and fosters connections within the U-M first-generation community.

Center for Education for Women+

CEW+ provides academic, financial, and professional support to help individuals overcome barriers and reach their full potential. Focused on empowering women and underserved communities, it offers counseling, funding, workshops, and a welcoming community to foster personal and academic growth.

International Center

Student Life’s International Center (IC) serves U-M's international student, faculty, scholar, and staff population, facilitates intercultural and international education, and fosters a global campus community at the University of Michigan and beyond. The IC is also the university's central source of advice and counsel on U.S. immigration regulations and compliance.

Office of Student Conflict Resolution

The Office of Student Conflict Resolution provides a variety of programs and services designed to support a safe, just, and peaceful campus community by helping U-M students learn how to manage and resolve conflicts.
